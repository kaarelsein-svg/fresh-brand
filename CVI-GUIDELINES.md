## 2. Logo

### 2.1 Primary Logo
The FRESH. wordmark uses custom geometric letterforms drawn with strokes, not fills. This gives the logo a technical, engineered quality — like a blueprint or wireframe.

- **File:** `assets/logo.svg`
- **Construction:** Mono-weight strokes, square line-caps, miter joins
- **The period** is a small square (not a circle) — representing precision and finality
- **Stroke weight:** 3px at default size

### 2.2 Logo Mark
A compact version featuring the **F.** letterform inside a thin rectangular frame.

- **File:** `assets/logo-mark.svg`
- **Usage:** Favicons, social media avatars, app icons, small applications

### 2.3 Clear Space
Maintain a minimum clear space equal to the height of the period mark on all sides.

### 2.4 Logo Don'ts
- Do not fill the letterforms
- Do not rotate or skew
- Do not change stroke weights
- Do not use drop shadows or gradients on the logo
- Do not enclose in shapes (except the mark version)

## 3. Color Palette

### 3.1 Primary Colors

| Name        | Hex       | RGB             | Usage                        |
|-------------|-----------|-----------------|------------------------------|
| Black       | `#0A0A0A` | 10, 10, 10      | Primary background           |
| White       | `#F0F0F0` | 240, 240, 240   | Primary text, logo on dark   |
| Accent      | `#C8FF00` | 200, 255, 0     | Highlights, CTAs, emphasis   |

### 3.2 Secondary / Neutral

| Name        | Hex       | Usage                        |
|-------------|-----------|------------------------------|
| Gray 100    | `#E0E0E0` | Secondary text               |
| Gray 300    | `#999999` | Body text on dark            |
| Gray 500    | `#666666` | Muted text, labels           |
| Gray 700    | `#333333` | Subtle borders               |
| Gray 900    | `#1A1A1A` | Card backgrounds, sections   |
| Accent Dim  | `#A3CC00` | Hover states on accent       |

### 3.3 Usage Rules
- The primary palette is **dark-first**. Light backgrounds are the exception.
- Accent color (`#C8FF00`) is used sparingly — for emphasis, interactive elements, and key data points.
- Never use accent as a background for large areas of text.

## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Primary Typeface
**Inter** — Used for all headings and body text.

| Style     | Weight | Size Range         | Usage                  |
|-----------|--------|--------------------|------------------------|
| Display   | 800    | 48–128px           | Hero headlines         |
| H1        | 800    | 40–88px            | Section titles         |
| H2        | 700    | 28–56px            | Sub-section titles     |
| H3        | 600    | 20–28px            | Card titles, labels    |
| Body Lg   | 400    | 18–22px            | Lead paragraphs        |
| Body      | 400    | 16px               | Standard body text     |
| Body Sm   | 400    | 14px               | Captions, footnotes    |

### 4.2 Monospace Typeface
**JetBrains Mono** — Used for labels, tags, section numbers, technical details.

| Style     | Weight | Size  | Usage                          |
|-----------|--------|-------|--------------------------------|
| Mono      | 400    | 12px  | Section labels, tags, numbers  |

### 4.3 Typography Rules
- Headlines use negative letter-spacing (`-0.03em` to `-0.01em`)
- Monospace labels are always uppercase with `0.05em` tracking
- Line heights: headings `0.95–1.2`, body `1.5–1.6`

## 5. Graphic Elements

### 5.1 Grid / Wireframe Motif
A subtle grid overlay (60px squares, ~4% opacity) references the technical/blueprint aesthetic. Used as background texture in hero sections.

### 5.2 Noise Texture
A film-grain noise overlay at 3% opacity sits over the entire page, adding organic warmth to the digital surface.

### 5.3 Glow Effect
A radial gradient glow (accent color at 4–6% opacity) creates ambient depth. Used behind hero content and follows cursor on desktop.

### 5.4 Scan Line
A thin horizontal line (1px, 25% opacity) runs along the bottom of the logo, referencing tech/terminal aesthetics.

### 5.5 Section Labels
Formatted as: `[ 001 — Section Name ]` using monospace type with accent-colored brackets.

---

## 6. Iconography
- Icons are constructed with the same stroke-based approach as the logo
- 1.5–2px stroke weight, square caps, no fills
- Accent color for emphasis states
- Keep icons geometric and minimal

---

## 7. Photography & Imagery
- Dark, high-contrast imagery preferred
- When using project mockups, apply dark overlays with gradient fade
- Gradient backgrounds use deep, muted tones (navy, charcoal, slate)
- Avoid stock photography that feels generic or corporate

---

## 8. Motion & Animation
- **Entrance:** Elements slide up 40px and fade in (`ease-out-expo`)
- **Hover:** Subtle lifts (`translateY(-2px)`) and color shifts to accent
- **Transitions:** 0.3–0.8s duration, primarily `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)`
- **Marquee:** Continuous horizontal scroll for capability tags
- **Counters:** Animated number counting with easeOutQuart easing

---

## 9. Voice & Tone
- **Confident** but not arrogant
- **Direct** — short sentences, no fluff
- **Slightly provocative** — we challenge conventions
- **Inclusive** — "we" language, inviting collaboration
- Copy should feel like talking to the smartest person at the party who's actually nice

### Examples:
- ✅ "We figure it out. Every time."
- ✅ "No playbook. That's the point."
- ❌ "We leverage synergies across verticals."
- ❌ "Your one-stop-shop for creative solutions."
